About Bike Points

Bike points are numbered intersections connected by well-signed bike routes. Together, they form a wayfinding network that can unify bicycle-friendly roads, paths, and other facilities.
They are already a popular and low-cost form of infrastructure in extensive use in the Netherlands.

Unifying Infrastructure

Bike points make navigation easy. Direction signs guide people safely from key-location to key-location, routing them away from busy and dangerous streets and towards existing bike infrastructure. Between key-locations, small but well-placed signs tell people where to turn and assure them that they are on the right paths to their destinations.

Lowering The Barrier to Bicycling

BikePoints show new bicyclists where the streets are safest. Incoming students use them to find their way around new cities. Tourists use them to explore the area’s attractions. Retirees use them to remain active. And everyone uses them for recreation.

Helping Local Businesses

Restaurants, ice cream parlors, museums, hotels, and campsites use BikePoitns as economic anchors, locating their stores nearby. BikePoints provide a steady stream of new hungry customers along their routes. In popular tourist regions, they are an engine of economic growth.

Why Install BikePoints

Community members love bike points. They are innexpensive. And they are difficult to object to. After installation, BikePoint wayfinding networks create political and economic pressure to improve bicycling infrastructure along their routes.
